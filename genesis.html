The Story Behind the Journal

Genesis

by Praveen Kumar
Chapter I
Eleven Years

For eleven years I have lived inside the world of horses. Stallions, warmbloods, creatures of instinct and grace. They remade me from the inside.

Horsemanship was never about force. It was learning to stand without fear and becoming the kind of man a thousand-pound animal chooses to trust.

I am ordinary. Quiet. But with horses, I could breathe. In their presence, life finally made sense.

For years I trained with hidden urgency. The horses obeyed, yet they did not fully rest in my presence.

Chapter II
The Black Stallion

Then the Black Stallion arrived. Four years old. Barely schooled. A living storm wrapped in midnight muscle.

He read me with an honesty that cut through every mask I wore.

The universe had not sent me a horse to train. It had sent me a mirror that refused to lie.

Inside a small fifteen-meter circle, fear lived quietly in both of us every day.

Chapter III
The Storm

He tried to run. I stopped him. He exploded forward and dragged me through the dirt.

Later I let him move freely. When the storm passed, he came back on his own.

I shouted one day, blamed him, and walked away. I thought I was done forever.

But I stayed.
Chapter IV
The Surrender

I stopped performing. I stopped correcting. I watched.

When I returned, I did not try to fix him. I stayed close, quiet, present.

A horse does not react to your hands first. He reacts to your inner state.

I began rewarding willingness instead of forcing response. He began changing on his own.

Chapter V
The Bond

Today, when he sees me, he calls out. He leans into me. He rests his head and exhales trust.

I thought I was training him. The truth is he was training me.

With him, I am finally clear. That has always been enough.

Eleven years of searching. One black stallion. One moment of surrender.

He did not just teach me how to train horses.
He taught me how to train myself.

I am exactly where I belong.

It is everything.
